**Course Title: Mastering Sales to High-Net-Worth Buyers – The Psychology, Frameworks, and Playbooks for Closing Premium Deals**

This comprehensive course transforms Codie Sanchez’s video “3 Rules For Selling To Rich People (Make A TON of Money)” into a structured, actionable learning program. It draws directly from her insights (based on her experience buying/selling businesses, working at Goldman Sachs selling $100M+ deals, and running Contrarian Thinking/Boardroom) while expanding them with context, real-world examples, nuances, psychological principles, case studies, exercises, edge cases, and implementation tools.

The core thesis: **Broke people buy on price. Rich (or high-achieving) people buy on risk mitigation, peace of mind, time savings, reputation protection, and control.** Leading with “affordable” or discounting signals low value and raises red flags. Instead, position yourself as a trusted ally who delivers certainty on expensive problems.

**Course Goals:**
- Shift your sales mindset from price competition to value/risk frameworks.
- Master diagnostics, buyer psychology, and closing techniques tailored to wealthy buyers.
- Build repeatable systems for higher-ticket sales, referrals, and long-term relationships.
- Apply across B2B services, products, consulting, SaaS, luxury goods, or business acquisitions.

**Who This Is For:** Entrepreneurs, salespeople, consultants, advisors, agency owners, or anyone selling high-value solutions (5-7+ figures ideally).

**Format & Duration:** Self-paced; ~8-12 modules. Each includes video/key concept review (reference the original ~28-min video), expanded explanations, examples, worksheets/exercises, and reflection prompts. Aim for 1-2 modules per week with real-world application.

### Module 1: Foundations – The Wealth Buyer Mindset Shift
**Key Concept:** Price is rarely the primary objection. Rich buyers prioritize **four currencies**: Time, Risk, Reputation, and Control.

- **Time:** They have money but limited bandwidth. Avoid wasting it with multiple calls or poor preparation.
- **Risk:** Every “yes” exposes them (reputation, operations, legal). Cheap = higher perceived risk (lawsuits, failure, compromise).
- **Reputation:** Decisions are scrutinized by peers, family, investors. Associations matter (e.g., buying a luxury watch from a reputable source to avoid fakes).
- **Control:** They built wealth through decision-making. Don’t box them in; give options and autonomy.

**Expanded Context & Nuances:** 
- Backed by Bain/Harvard Business Review insights on B2B buyers valuing reduced anxiety and expertise over pure cost savings.
- Edge case: Ultra-high-net-worth individuals (UHNW) vs. “aspirational rich” – the former may be more skeptical due to constant pitches.
- Example: Codie’s Boardroom program – members care about outcomes and time, not the five-figure price, because doubling a $1M+ business dwarfs the cost.

**Exercises:**
1. Audit your current pitch: Count how often you mention price/discounts vs. risk mitigation.
2. List 3-5 problems you solve in terms of the four currencies.

**Implications:** Reframing turns sales from transactional to consultative. Measure success by deal size and close rate, not volume.

### Module 2: The Core Principle – Risk Mitigation Over Price
**Deep Dive:** “Broke buyers ask ‘Can I afford this?’ Rich buyers ask ‘Will this fix my problem and is it valuable enough?’”

- Stop apologizing for price; it signals weakness.
- Sell peace of mind, certainty, and transformation (e.g., “never think about this problem again”).
- Common pitfall: Discounting prematurely kills perceived value.

**Examples & Case Studies:**
- Luxury brands thrive on reputation (vintage watch buying in Paris).
- Business context: Helping scale from $1M to $2M+ revenue makes price irrelevant.
- Counter-example: Vendors who need “six calls to understand the assignment” get ghosted.

**Practical Tools:** Value equation worksheet (Problem Severity × Risk Exposure × Time Savings / Your Solution Certainty).

**Edge Cases:** Economic downturns – risk aversion heightens; emphasize guarantees, testimonials, and low-friction onboarding.

### Module 3: The EGO Framework – Earn, Gap, Outcome
**Core Framework:** Avoid ego clashes. Compliment achievements first, then collaboratively diagnose gaps, and paint outcomes. Turn buyer into ally, not adversary.

- **Earn:** Acknowledge what they’ve built (“You’ve scaled impressively to $X...”).
- **Gap:** Diagnose collaboratively (“What’s holding back the next level?”).
- **Outcome:** Focus on results and transformation.

**Why It Works:** Successful people have big egos. Attacking their current setup triggers defense. Be an expert diagnostician, not a critic.

**Expanded Application:**
- Scripts and role-plays.
- Authority shift: Stop describing features (“We’re affordable”); diagnose like a doctor.
- Nuances: Adapt for cultural differences or introverted buyers.

**Exercise:** Record a mock sales call using EGO; self-critique or get feedback.

### Module 4: Stop Describing, Start Diagnosing – Authority Positioning
Build on Module 3. Ask probing questions to demonstrate expertise and uncover real pain.

- Techniques: Open-ended questions, active listening, consequence anchoring (focus on cost of inaction).
- Authority signals: Case studies, proprietary insights, credentials without bragging.

**Implications:** Positions you as peer/partner, not vendor. Increases trust and willingness to pay premium.

### Module 5: Buyer Typology – Optimizer, Delegator, Rationalizer
Tailor closes to personality.

- **Optimizer:** Data-driven; wants proof, metrics, comparisons.
- **Delegator:** Busy; values time savings, white-glove service, trust in your execution.
- **Rationalizer:** Needs to feel smart/justified; social proof, stories, control via options.

**Different Closes for Each:** Provide tailored recommendations, data packs, or decision frameworks.

**Exercise:** Classify past prospects and analyze what worked/didn’t.

**Edge Cases:** Hybrid types or buyers influenced by advisors/gatekeepers.

### Module 6: The Premium Close – Options, Recommendation, Reason, Next Step
**Framework:** Give control back. Present options, recommend one, explain why, and ask for next step.

- Say the price and shut up (silence is powerful).
- Anchor on value/consequence, not cost.

**Role-Play Drills:** Practice handling objections without discounting.

### Module 7: Follow-Up Mastery – Point, Proof, Path
Replace “just checking in” with value-driven sequences.

- **Point:** Specific insight/relevant update.
- **Proof:** Evidence or case study.
- **Path:** Clear next action.

**System:** Multi-touch sequences (email, call, LinkedIn) with templates. Track response rates.

**Long-Term:** Build referral engines – the real prize is trust, not one-off money.
