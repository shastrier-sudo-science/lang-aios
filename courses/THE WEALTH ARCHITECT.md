MODULE 0.2: THE COMPOUNDING THEORY — MONEY AS AMPLIFIED TIME
The Core Principle
Time compounds relentlessly. Waste 30 minutes scrolling → you’re tired later, less productive tomorrow, smaller results this month, missed opportunities this year, a different (smaller) life in a decade.
Money does the exact same thing.
Spend $5 frivolously today → that $5 (plus what it could have earned) is gone forever. Over decades, that repeated choice creates a dramatically poorer version of you.
Save and invest that same $5 wisely → it works while you sleep, earns returns on returns, and funds freedom later.
The Time-Money Mirror Framework
Dimension	Time Analogy	Money Parallel	Positive Action	Negative Trap	Compounding Multiplier
Daily	30 min wasted	$5-20 impulse spend	Invest 1 hour/skill or $	Small leaks/debt interest	Slow build or erosion
Monthly	Missed goals	Low savings rate	Automate savings + doubling moves	Lifestyle creep	Trajectory setter
Yearly	Stagnant skills	Missed investing	Layer assets (business + markets)	Chronic under-earning	Freedom or struggle
Decades	Different life path	Missed early investing	Estate planning + influence	No protection	Dynasties or burdens
Positive vs. Negative Compounding
Positive Compounding (The Wealth Snowball): - High savings rate + automatic investing - High-return assets (business equity, index funds) - Reinvestment (never interrupt unnecessarily) - Skills & influence (compounding into higher income) - Good habits (health, networks, learning)
Negative Compounding (The Poverty Avalanche): - Debt interest compounds against you (credit cards at 20%+) - Lifestyle creep or small daily leaks - Missed opportunities (not learning skills, not starting) - Losses hurt more than gains help: 50% loss requires 100% gain to break even
The Rule of 72
Divide 72 by your annual return rate = years to double. - 10% return? ~7.2 years per double - 20% return? ~3.6 years per double - Focus on the NEXT double, not the total
Your Compounding Policy (Write This Down)
“I treat every dollar and hour as a seed for my future self. I review quarterly. I choose positive exponential growth.”

MODULE 0.3: CREATIVE THINKING & PROBLEM-SOLVING AS WEALTH TOOLS
Why This Matters for You
You have 3 hours daily. You have $0 budget. You need to solve problems others will PAY to have solved. Creative thinking isn’t a “nice to have”—it’s your competitive advantage.
The Frameworks
1. Divergent Thinking (Fluency, Flexibility, Originality, Elaboration)
Generate many ideas without initial judgment. Classic test: 50+ alternative uses for a paperclip.
Business Application: When stuck on “how do I make money,” generate 50 ideas in 15 minutes. Don’t judge. Then converge on the best 3.
2. Lateral Thinking (Edward de Bono)
Approach problems indirectly. Challenge assumptions. Use provocation or random stimuli.
Business Application: Instead of “how do I get more customers,” ask “what if customers came to me because I solved a problem no one else sees?”
3. SCAMPER (Innovation Checklist)
	Substitute: What can you replace?
	Combine: What can you merge?
	Adapt: What can you borrow from another industry?
	Modify/Magnify/Minify: What can you change size, shape, or scale?
	Put to another use: What other contexts work?
	Eliminate: What can you remove?
	Reverse/Rearrange: What if you flip the order?
Business Application: Take any existing business model. Apply SCAMPER to create your unique version.
4. Design Thinking (Human-Centered)
Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test
Business Application: Don’t build what YOU think people need. Find 10 people with a real problem. Build the smallest solution. Test. Iterate.
5. The 5 Whys (Root Cause Analysis)
Ask “why” five times to get to the root of any problem.
Business Application: - “Why am I broke?” → “Because I don’t have enough income.” - “Why don’t I have enough income?” → “Because I haven’t built a revenue engine.” - “Why haven’t I built a revenue engine?” → “Because I keep analyzing instead of executing.” - “Why do I keep analyzing?” → “Because I’m afraid of failure.” - “Why am I afraid of failure?” → “Because I tie my worth to outcomes instead of identity.”
Root cause: Identity attachment to outcomes. Solution: Separate identity from outcome. Execute as experiment.

SCAMPER on Problem: Apply to “how to make money with no budget” 17. Crazy 8s: 8 quick solutions to “how to reach $1K/month” in 8 minutes 18. Mid-Course Review: Pick favorite 3 ideas. Question: What’s the smallest version that delivers value? - One feature - One customer segment - One pricing tier
Phase 4: Security Review
Question: What are the risks? - Financial risk - Reputation risk - Time risk - Legal risk
Phase 5: Market Proof
Question: Is anyone already paying for something similar? - Search for competitors - Check if similar products have revenue - Look for “spreadsheet signals” (people sharing Google Sheets/Notion templates)
Phase 6: Chairman’s Verdict
Question: Based on all evidence, GO or NO-GO? - If GO: One next action, today - If NO-GO: Kill it fast. Pivot.

	Can’t get 3 people to say “I’d pay for that” in 2 weeks
	Requires more than $100 to start
	Requires more than 3 hours daily to maintain
	No clear path to $1K/month in 90 days

MODULE 3.1: THE 7 BUSINESS METRICS THAT BUILD REAL WEALTH (Dan Martell)
The Core Principle
Build a business you could sell—even if you never do. This forces better systems, margins, and freedom.
Metric 1: Enterprise Value
Formula: Annual Profit × Industry Multiple Example: $500K profit at 3x multiple = $1.5M value Action: Audit if your business collapses without you
Metric 2: Gross Margin
Formula: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100 Target: 60%+ for digital products, 70%+ for SaaS Action: Calculate current margin. Identify 2-3 ways to improve.
Metric 3: Churn Rate
Formula: (Customers Lost ÷ Total Customers) × 100 (monthly) Target: <5% monthly for SaaS, <10% for coaching Action: Calculate monthly churn. Set goal to cut in half.
Metric 4: Lifetime Value (LTV)
Formula: (Monthly Revenue per Customer) ÷ Monthly Churn Rate Example: $100/month at 2% churn = $5,000 LTV Action: Compute LTV for main customer segments.
Metric 5: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
Formula: Total Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers Acquired Rule: CAC should pay back in <3 months Action: Track last 10-20 customers’ acquisition costs.
Metric 6: Conversion Rate
Formula: (Closed Deals ÷ Leads) × 100 Action: Map customer journey. Calculate stage-by-stage conversion. Fix weakest step.
Metric 7: Runway / Burn Rate
Formula: Cash in Bank ÷ Monthly Burn Rate Action: Know your cash buffer. Adjust aggression based on runway.
Your One-Page Business Health Dashboard
Create a simple spreadsheet tracking all 7 metrics weekly.
